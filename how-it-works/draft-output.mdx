At the end of your session, every confirmed and refined answer is compiled into a single markdown file at the root of your project. This is your working draft — something you own, can edit directly, and return to across multiple sessions. It's not a final submission and it's not a polished document. It's a structured record of your best current answers, ready for you to test, challenge, and improve before you paste anything into the YC form.

## The output file

The coach saves your draft to **`yc-application-draft.md`** at your project root. The file is created automatically when you complete a session and the coach does its final compilation pass.

### File format

```markdown
# YC Application Draft
# Company: [name]
# Last updated: [date]
# Status: Work in progress — review before submitting

---

## [Question text]

[Refined answer]

---

## [Question text]

[Refined answer]

---
```

Each section is separated by a horizontal rule and labeled with the exact YC question text so you know exactly which answer maps to which form field. The file updates each time you complete a session — confirmed answers from previous sessions are preserved and new ones are appended.

## What to do with your draft

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the whole thing out loud">
    Every sentence. Not skimming — out loud. Your ear catches what your eye skips. Any sentence you stumble on, rush through, or feel the need to mentally annotate while reading is a sentence that needs work. If you can't say it naturally, a YC partner won't believe it either.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Flag anything you can't elaborate on">
    For each answer, ask yourself: if a YC partner asked a follow-up question about this right now — no notes, no prep — could you answer it specifically? If not, that's what needs more work. Mark it, note what's missing, and come back in your next session.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Iterate across sessions">
    You own this file. Edit it directly between sessions. Add specifics when you gather new evidence. Rewrite sentences that feel off. Return to the coach for another pass on the sections you've updated. The draft gets sharper each time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Paste into the YC application form">
    When you're satisfied that every answer is specific, honest, and something you can defend without notes, paste them into the official YC application form at [ycombinator.com/apply](https://www.ycombinator.com/apply). Don't copy-paste blindly — read each answer one more time in the form context to make sure it still sounds right.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Get a human review

Before you submit, get a second set of eyes from someone who has actually been through the YC process. Submit your draft to **[YC Roaster](https://ycroaster.com)** for free feedback from a real YC alum. You'll get the same direct, evidence-demanding pushback you got in the coaching session — from someone who knows what YC partners look for because they've sat across from them.

If the reviewer sees strong potential, they may invite you for a 1-on-1 session to go deeper before you submit.

<Note>
  The draft is a starting point, not a finished product. The header says "Work in progress — review before submitting" because that's exactly what it is. Treat it as a living document. The best applications go through multiple rounds of revision, not because the first draft was bad, but because clarity improves with repetition.
</Note>

<Warning>
  The coach flags AI-sounding phrases with `[sounds generated — rewrite this in your own words]`. Fix every flagged phrase before you submit. YC partners read thousands of applications per batch and can identify generated language on sight — polished phrasing with no specific details, startup jargon used correctly but without grounding, sentences that could appear in any startup's application. A single flagged phrase you leave in can undermine the credibility of answers you worked hard to get right.
</Warning>
